Beyond the Game

Evaluating Environmental and Psychological Variables in Collegiate Athletics

Overview

This graduate research examined how environmental, psychological, and situational factors influence athletic performance and predictive modeling in collegiate sports.

Research Questions

  • How do environmental factors affect predictive accuracy?
  • How do psychological variables impact athlete performance?
  • How can these variables improve predictive models?

Methodology

  • Survey Design
  • Data Collection from Collegiate Athletes
  • Statistical Analysis in R
  • Principal Component Analysis (PCA)
  • Correlation Analysis
  • Literature Review

Key Findings

Travel Fatigue

Athletes identified travel fatigue as the most influential external performance factor.

Weather Impact

Weather conditions were found to significantly influence preparation and performance.

Prediction Limitations

Most athletes believed traditional statistics cannot fully predict game-day performance.
